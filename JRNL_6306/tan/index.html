Punishment and Restoration in US Justice System

While the Massachusetts RISE Program has been successful in providing an alternative to incarceration and a path to rehabilitation for some, restorative justice has a long way to go before becoming a viable solution. Resources are limited, skeptics are common and few of those who might be helped by restorative justice gets a chance to participate.

For everything from non-violent drug offenses to homicide, the U.S. justice system has one broad tool to address crime: incarceration. The U.S. prison population has thus ballooned exponentially. According to The Sentencing Project, there are 2 million people in U.S. prisons and jails, a 500% increase over the last 40 years. The U.S. accounts for 5% of the global population but more than 20% of the world's prison population, according to the American Civil Liberties Union.
